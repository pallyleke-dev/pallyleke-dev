The Brief

Where FreeCAD deals in mechanical assemblies and BlenderBIM handles buildings, Rhino 3D lives in a different territory — complex freeform surfaces, industrial design, and precision mesh geometry. Annotating this class of model required a different mindset entirely.

The task was to annotate 3D surface models and parametric constraint geometries so that AI systems could learn to identify geometric primitives — vertices, edges, faces, curves — from raw 3D data. The models ranged from organic freeform shapes to highly constrained mechanical profiles built in SolveSpace.

The Process

Step 01
Model Analysis
Each model was analysed to understand its topology — how many faces, the curvature profile, key vertices, and constraint relationships in SolveSpace models.
Step 02
Vertex Identification
Key vertices — apex points, base vertices, inflection points, and junction nodes — were identified and labelled with their spatial coordinates and geometric role.
Step 03
Edge Classification
Each edge was classified by type — sharp crease, soft blend, silhouette, or boundary — and tagged with its parent surface and connecting vertices.
Step 04
Face Labelling
Surface patches and mesh faces were labelled by geometry type — planar, cylindrical, conical, toroidal, or freeform NURBS — and linked to their bounding edge loops.
Step 05
Constraint Annotation (SolveSpace)
In SolveSpace models, parametric constraints — distances, angles, coincident points, parallel edges — were extracted and documented as part of the annotation manifest.
Step 06
Export & QA
Annotated models were exported with full label manifests. Geometric consistency checks verified that all vertex coordinates and face normals were correctly documented.

Challenges & Solutions

Freeform topology. Unlike mechanical CAD where every edge has a clear engineering purpose, freeform Rhino surfaces can have thousands of control points and no obvious semantic structure. Developing a consistent labelling vocabulary for organic geometry required building a custom annotation guide specific to this model class.

SolveSpace constraint extraction. SolveSpace stores constraints as abstract mathematical relationships — not as visible geometry. Extracting and documenting these constraints in a human-readable annotation format required parsing the .slvs file format directly.
